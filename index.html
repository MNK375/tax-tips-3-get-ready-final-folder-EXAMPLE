We’re all happy to welcome the new year, but 2020 isn’t really behind you until your taxes are filed.

Filing this year might be more complicated, but we’ve rounded up the latest in tax news so you can get the most out of your return.
 
     
 
How COVID-19 relief measures could change your taxes this year.
 
Though some of the emergency benefits created in 2020 are tax free, it’s important to know which federal and provincial relief programs could impact your income on your 2020 return.
 
Learn More. 
 
     
 
Claim your expenses while working from home.
 
Employees working from home due to COVID-19 can use a simplified method of claiming expenses, based on the number of days working from home.
 
Learn More. 
 
     
 
Supplemental Canada Child Benefit Payments.
 
Parents with children under age six could receive additional child benefit payments in 2021. Supplemental payments of up to $300 will be made quarterly throughout 2021.
 
Learn More. 
 
     
 
Disability tax credits Canadians need to know.
 
Disabled taxpayers often have unavoidable expenses that others don’t have to face. To offset these costs, Canadians with disabilities are eligible for unique tax credits and deductions.
 
Learn More. 
 
     
 
2021 employee stock option updates.
 
The new rules for employee stock options will be implemented in July 2021, after originally being proposed in the 2016 budget.
 
Learn More. 
 
     
 
Enhancements to Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy (CEWS).
 
Business owners can expect more support from the federal government in 2021. Learn about increases to the wage subsidy and more.
 
Learn More. 
 
     
 
Extension of Canada Emergency Rent Subsidy (CERS).
 
Lockdowns and continued closures are affecting business owners all over Canada. Learn more about how the extended CERS is helping small business owners.
 
Learn More. 
 
     
 
Saskatchewan Home Renovation Tax Credit.
 
Hoping to renovate? The provincial government has released details of its new Home Renovation Tax Credit. Eligible projects starting from October 2020 can now be claimed in 2021.
 
Learn More.
